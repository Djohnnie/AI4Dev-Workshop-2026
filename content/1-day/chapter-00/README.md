# Chapter 0 — Introduction

> **Duration:** Pre-workshop | Day 1, before 09:00

The opening chapter sets the stage for the one-day **AI4Dev** workshop — *develop smarter with AI without losing control*. It introduces your trainer, shares an honest, critical stance on GenAI tooling, sets expectations through a transparency disclaimer, lets the room introduce itself, and walks through the full day's agenda.

---

## 🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter you will:

- Know who your trainer is and where to find all workshop resources.
- Understand the workshop's balanced, critical position on GenAI — neither rejecting it instantly nor adopting it blindly.
- Appreciate why transparency and accountability matter when using AI to produce work.
- Share your background and goals so the training can be tailored to the room.
- Have a clear picture of what the day will cover, chapter by chapter.

## 📋 Content Outline

### 1. AI4Dev — Welcome

- The workshop theme: **develop smarter with AI, without losing control**.

### 2. Who Is Your Trainer?

- **Johnny Hooyberghs** — focus on .NET custom application development, Azure Cloud, and GenAI.
- 18 years of professional experience as a software enthusiast; interested in Quantum Computing.
- Public speaker and trainer; **Microsoft MVP** in Developer Technologies and Microsoft Azure since 2020.

### 3. Where to Find the Resources

- All slides, exercises, and labs live in the workshop GitHub repository: `https://github.com/Djohnnie/AI4Dev-Workshop-2026`.

### 4. My Stance on GenAI Tooling

- *Skeptical two years ago. Still skeptical today. More convinced by practical value.*
- **Two years ago:** real doubts about the hype, the quality, and the risk of trading craftsmanship for convenience.
- **Today:** still doubts, but also clear evidence these tools can speed up delivery and help produce high-quality results.
- **Our responsibility:** show management what works, what does not, and where human judgment still matters most.
- Bottom line: do not reject GenAI instantly, do not adopt it blindly — use it critically, measure the outcome, and keep accountability with the team.

### 5. A Quick Disclaimer

- *GenAI was used to build this course — deliberately, transparently, and one artifact at a time.*
- **Where:** to help create these slides and parts of some exercises and labs.
- **How:** not by generating everything in bulk, but by prompting for one slide, one lab, or one idea at a time to move faster.
- **What still matters:** transparency and responsibility — the trainer still reviews, verifies, and owns the result.
- Using AI is not the problem; hiding it or outsourcing accountability is.

### 6. Who Are You? (Audience Intro)

A round-the-room introduction so the training can adapt to the audience. Each participant shares:

- First name and function title.
- How they are using GenAI today.
- Whether they are using GitHub Copilot today.
- What they want to learn from the workshop.

### 7. Day 1 — What Is on the Agenda?

- **Chapter 1 — Welcome to the AI Revolution & Power with Purpose** — AI foundations, GitHub Copilot positioning, responsible use, privacy, and what it means for developers.
- **Chapter 2 — Meet Your New Best Friend & Let It Take the Wheel** — completions, chat, agent mode, Copilot CLI, and GitHub.com features from zero to confident daily use.
- **Chapter 3 — Speak AI's Language: Mastering Prompts, Workflow & Best Practices** — structured prompting, context variables, workflow integration, and team best practices.
- **Chapter 4 — Get Your Hands Dirty: Real-World AI in Action** — capstone recap and a full end-to-end build with Copilot as a partner across the whole lifecycle.
