# Chapter 0 — Introduction

> **Duration:** Pre-workshop | Day 1, before 09:00

The opening chapter sets the stage for the two-day **AI4Dev** workshop — *develop smarter with AI without losing control*. It introduces your trainer, shares an honest, critical stance on GenAI tooling, sets expectations through a transparency disclaimer, gets every laptop set up with the required tools, lets the room introduce itself, and walks through the Day 1 and Day 2 agendas.

---

## 🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter you will:

- Know who your trainer is and where to find all workshop resources.
- Understand the workshop's balanced, critical position on GenAI — neither rejecting it instantly nor adopting it blindly.
- Appreciate why transparency and accountability matter when using AI to produce work.
- Have a working development environment with the **.NET 10 SDK** and **Visual Studio Code** installed and verified.
- Share your background and goals so the training can be tailored to the room.
- Have a clear picture of what Day 1 and Day 2 will cover, chapter by chapter.

### 3. Where to Find the Resources

### 4. My Stance on GenAI Tooling

- *Skeptical two years ago. Still skeptical today. More convinced by practical value.*
- **Two years ago:** real doubts about the hype, the quality, and the risk of trading craftsmanship for convenience.
- **Today:** still doubts, but also clear evidence these tools can speed up delivery and help produce high-quality results.
- **Our responsibility:** show management what works, what does not, and where human judgment still matters most.
- Bottom line: do not reject GenAI instantly, do not adopt it blindly — use it critically, measure the outcome, and keep accountability with the team.

### 5. A Quick Disclaimer

- *GenAI was used to build this course — deliberately, transparently, and one artifact at a time.*
- **Where:** to help create these slides and parts of some exercises and labs.
- **How:** not by generating everything in bulk, but by prompting for one slide, one lab, or one idea at a time to move faster.
- **What still matters:** transparency and responsibility — the trainer still reviews, verifies, and owns the result.
- Using AI is not the problem; hiding it or outsourcing accountability is.

### 6. Before We Start — Set Up Your Laptop

Download and install the following tools — both are **free** and available for Windows, macOS, and Linux.

- **.NET 10 SDK** — runtime, compiler, and CLI tools for building .NET applications.
  - Download at `https://dot.net/download`; choose the SDK installer for your OS.
  - Verify with `dotnet --version`.
- **Visual Studio Code** — lightweight editor with built-in Git, extensions, and AI coding support.
  - Download at `https://code.visualstudio.com`; use the stable release for your OS.
  - Verify with `code --version`.
