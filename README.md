# AI for Developers Workshop 2026

> *Hands-on. Practical. No fluff.*

📋 **Before the workshop:** Please fill in the [Developer AI Readiness Survey](SURVEY.md) — it takes ~10 minutes and helps us tailor the sessions to where you actually are.

Developers today are being asked to work alongside AI tools that can write, review, test, and explain code — often faster than a junior colleague. This workshop cuts through the hype and gives you the foundation to understand what these tools actually are, how GitHub Copilot fits into your daily workflow, and how to use it confidently and responsibly across every phase of the software development lifecycle.

Whether you join us for a full day or two, you will leave with working knowledge, practical techniques, and a personal action plan you can start applying the very next morning.

#### Chapter Descriptions

**Chapter 1 — Welcome to the AI Revolution!**
We start by cutting through the noise: AI is not one thing, and knowing the difference between machine learning, large language models, and generative AI matters for how you use these tools day to day. We then tackle the question every developer should ask before adopting any AI tool: what are the guardrails? You will understand how Copilot handles your code, where bias and hallucination creep in, and how to apply a practical responsible-AI checklist so you can ship with confidence.

**Chapter 2 — Let Your AI Co-Pilot Take the Wheel**
This session is pure hands-on productivity. You will set up GitHub Copilot in VS Code and go from your first autocomplete to full Agent Mode in a single session — learning when to use inline completions, when to open Chat, when to switch models, and how Agent Mode can plan and execute multi-file changes while you review. Custom Instructions and the Copilot Edits workflow round out a toolkit that will feel instantly useful when you are back at your desk.

**Chapter 3 — Speak AI's Language: Mastering Prompts, Workflow & Best Practices**
Great prompts are not magic words — they are engineering. You will learn how Copilot builds context from your open files and cursor position, how to write prompts that constrain and guide the model, and which context variables give you precision without extra effort. The second half applies these skills to your wider workflow: the Think → Prompt → Review → Iterate loop, using Copilot as a code reviewer, and measuring whether AI is actually making you faster.

**Chapter 4 — Get Your Hands Dirty: Real-World AI in Action**
The final session ties everything together. A rapid tour shows Copilot applied to testing, documentation, debugging, and pull-request descriptions — the everyday SDLC tasks where it pays back the most. Then comes the centrepiece: a focused 40-minute build sprint where you take a real feature from brief to merged PR using every tool from the day. You will demo what you built, hear what others discovered, and leave with a written action plan for the next 30 days.

#### Chapter Descriptions

**Chapter 1 — Welcome to the AI Revolution!**
A grounding session on the AI landscape: the difference between narrow AI, machine learning, and large language models; how foundation models are trained and what that means for their outputs; and where generative AI sits in a developer's toolbox. Participants leave with a shared vocabulary and a clear mental model of what GitHub Copilot actually is under the hood.

**Chapter 2 — Meet Your New Best Friend: GitHub Copilot**
The full setup-and-completions session: configuring Copilot in VS Code, understanding the context window, and mastering inline completions before moving into Chat. You will explore all the built-in Chat participants (`@workspace`, `@vscode`, `@terminal`), slash commands (`/explain`, `/fix`, `/tests`, `/doc`), and learn when to reach for Completions versus Chat for maximum precision.

**Chapter 3 — Power with Purpose: Using AI Responsibly**
A dedicated deep-dive into the responsible-AI dimension. Topics include how Copilot protects (and does not protect) your code, where bias lives in foundation models, the IP and licensing landscape, and a full responsible-AI checklist workshop. Teams also discuss disclosure norms and how to decide what is safe to share with a cloud-hosted model — a conversation every engineering team needs to have.

**Chapter 4 — Let Your AI Co-Pilot Take the Wheel**
The full Agent Mode and advanced tooling session. Where Chapter 2 covered the foundations, this chapter goes further: Agent Mode for multi-step, multi-file tasks; Copilot Edits for broad refactors; the Copilot CLI for terminal-first workflows; model switching in the Chat panel; and Custom Instructions at the file, project, and organisation level.

**Chapter 5 — Speak AI's Language: Mastering Prompts & Context**
The full prompt-engineering session. Every context variable is covered (`#file`, `#selection`, `#git`, `#sym`, `#codebase`), chain-of-thought and diff-driven prompting are introduced, and participants work through a structured prompting exercise that builds from a basic request to a well-constrained, context-rich instruction they can reuse immediately.

**Chapter 6 — AI Across the Entire Software Lifecycle**
A systematic walkthrough of every SDLC phase where Copilot adds measurable value: writing and extending tests with `/tests`, generating and maintaining documentation with `/doc`, debugging with error-paste and `/fix`, refactoring for readability and performance, and using Copilot throughout the pull-request lifecycle. Each phase includes a live demo and a short hands-on exercise.

**Chapter 7 — Level Up: Best Practices for AI-Powered Development**
The workflow-and-culture session. The Think → Prompt → Review → Iterate loop, using Copilot as a code-review partner, measuring productivity impact with real data from GitHub's research, team norms and disclosure policies, and a look at the tools — Knowledge Bases, Extensions, MCP — that go beyond the core product. Participants end with a personal development plan for continued growth.

**Chapter 8 — Get Your Hands Dirty: Real-World AI in Action**
The two-day capstone. Teams self-select a project (REST API, data CLI, or front-end component), work through four timed sprints covering design, implementation, testing, and documentation plus PR, then present their results in a structured show-and-tell. The session closes with a retrospective, individual action plans, and a look at what is coming next in the Copilot roadmap.
